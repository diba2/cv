Eliud Kipchoge

The greatest marathoner of the modern era.

Biography

Eliud Kipchoge (born 5 November 1984 in Kapsisywa, Nandi District, is kenyan long distance runner who has won medals at Olympic and World level.

Career

2002 - 2004

In 2002, he won at the Kenyan trials for the 2002 IAAF World Cross Country Championships junior race. He set a world junior record in the 5000 m at the 2003 Bislett Games. In July he participated in the Golden League 2004 Roma Meeting.

2006 - 2009

He won the bronze in the 3000 metres indoor at the 2006 World Championships in Moscow. In 2007 he won a silver medal at the 5000 m final World Championships at Osaka.

2010 - 2013

In 2010 he won 5000 m IAAF Diamond League in Qatar. In Early 2011, he won the short race at the Great Edinburgh Cross Country, ahead of Asbel Kiprop. In October 2012 Kipchoge run in the 2012 IAAF World Half Marathon Championships in Kavarna Bulgaria. In 2013 he won Barcelona Half Marathon in a time of one hour and four seconds.

2015 - 2019

Kipchoge won the Berlin Marathon in 2015 and in 2016 April, he won the London Marathon. On 24 September 2017, he won the Berlin Marathon.
